What Is This Companion?

This companion documents UNESCO's entire Treasury operations domain in SAP — from opening a new bank account to processing payments, importing bank statements, and reporting cash positions. It is the single reference for anyone who needs to configure, operate, or audit house banks in UNESCO's SAP landscape.

Who uses this: DBS/BFM configurators (house bank setup), Treasury operators (F110 payments, FEBAN reconciliation), auditors (OBA1 valuation, BCM dual-control), and support analysts (EBS troubleshooting).

Sources: 45-page handover PDF (Marlies Spronk), Gold DB analysis of 211 house banks (P01, 24K+ config rows across 11 tables), UBA01 real configuration (2026-04-07/08, 7 issues found and fixed), ECO09 benchmark comparison, and 3 independent validation agents.

How a House Bank Request Arrives

Every house bank configuration starts with an email from Treasury (TRS) or Budget & Financial Management (BFM). The email includes Excel forms and a bank confirmation letter. The forms drive EVERYTHING — each field maps to a specific configuration step.

Two Scenarios

ScenarioWhat ArrivesSteps Required
A: New Bank
UNESCO opens account at a new bank (e.g., UBA in Mozambique)		 • 1 House bank form per account ID
• 2 G/L forms (bank 10* + clearing 11*) per account
• Bank confirmation letter (IBAN, SWIFT, address)		 ALL 13 steps
Bank directory (BNKA), house bank master (T012), G/L creation (FS00), OBA1, EBS, GS02, etc.
B: New Account on Existing Bank
Add a new currency account to an existing bank (e.g., add EUR to UBA01)		 • 1 House bank form for the new account ID
• 2 G/L forms (bank + clearing)
• Bank letter with new account number		 Steps 2-13 only
Bank already exists in T012/BNKA. Add new T012K entry, new G/Ls, new OBA1/EBS/GS02 entries for the new currency.

The 3 Input Documents

House Bank Form (.xls) — 1 per account ID
Naming: {HBKID}-{AcctID}.xls (e.g., UBA01-MZN01.xls)
Contains: house bank ID, account ID, bank account number, currency, SWIFT, bank country, bank key, cash management name, EBS flag, replenishment flag
G/L Creation Form (Form AM3-11, .xls) — 1 per G/L account
Naming: Form AM3-11 - {GL}-{HBKID}-{AcctID}.xls
Contains: account number (10* or 11*), reference GL to copy from, account group (Bank a/c), currency, texts, house bank ID, "GL to be revaluated" flag
Bank Confirmation Letter (PDF) — 1 per bank
From: the bank itself
Contains: physical account numbers, IBAN, SWIFT/BIC code, bank name, address, authorized signatories

13 Configuration Steps — Full Detail

Source: 45-page handover PDF (Marlies Spronk) + UBA01 real configuration (2026-04-07) + Gold DB analysis of 211 banks. Each step includes the SAP transaction, table fields, form source, and lessons learned.

For a new bank: ALL steps apply. For a new account on existing bank: Skip step 1 (bank already exists), start at step 2.

Form source: G/L Creation Form (AM3-11). One form per G/L account. The "GL account to use as reference" field tells MD which existing GL to copy field values from.

Step 4 — OBA1: Exchange Rate Differences (T030H) ⚠

What: Tell SAP which P&L accounts to use when FX rate changes create gains or losses. Required for ALL non-USD G/L accounts — both bank (10*) AND clearing (11*). See the OBA1 section below for the 3 scenarios and complete template.

Form trigger: "Account Currency" != USD AND "GL to be revaluated" = YES.

CRITICAL RULE: Fill ALL 5 fields in a single pass. UBA01 required 3 transports because OBA1 was filled partially each time. Use the T030H template in the OBA1 section below.

Steps 5-6 — T035D + T028B: EBS Integration (same SPRO screen, two tables)

What: Two configurations in the same SPRO screen that together enable Electronic Bank Statement processing. Both are required — if either is missing, MT940 files from the bank will be rejected.

Required for: All EBS_CONFIG banks (form: "Bank statement electronically uploaded?" = Yes).

Step 5: T035D — Cash Management Account Name

Maps the house bank + account to a G/L. This is the "phone book" that tells SAP which GL to post to when a statement arrives.

Step 6: T028B — Bank Account to Transaction Type

Maps the physical bank account (by bank key + account number) to the statement format and links to T035D. This is how SAP identifies which bank sent the MT940 file.

FieldValueSource
BANKLBank key (e.g., SP0000001YCB)From T012. NOT HBKID!

If missing: FF_5 cannot identify the bank → MT940 file rejected. UBA01 lesson: T028B was initially missed because the compliance checker searched by HBKID (wrong key). T028B is keyed by BANKL + KTONR.

The bridge: T028B.BNKKO must EXACTLY match T035D.DISKB. This is how the two tables are linked. The chain is: MT940 file → T028B (identifies bank) → T035D (finds GL) → posting to 10xxxxx.

Step 7 — YFI_BASU_MOD: Business Area Substitution

Usually N/A. G/L range 1000000-1199999 is covered by a wildcard rule (Z1 AUTOMATIC BANK STAT / GEF). New bank accounts in this range are automatically assigned the correct business area. Only IIEP (10*=PDK, 11*=PAR) and UBO (10*=PFF) need individual entries.

OBA1 — T030H Exchange Rate Difference Configuration

Required for ALL non-USD G/L accounts (both bank 10* and clearing 11*). OBA1 tells SAP which P&L accounts to use when FX rate changes create gains or losses. There are 3 scenarios, each mapped to specific T030H fields:

Scenario 1: Realized FX Differences (LSREA / LHREA)

When: An open item posted at exchange rate X is cleared at rate Y (e.g., FEBAN clears a bank statement line against a payment document). The difference is a realized FX gain or loss that must be booked to the income statement.

FieldOBA1 LabelUNESCO ValueMeaning
LSREAExch.rate diff. realized — Loss6045011FX loss on clearing → P&L expense
LHREAExch.rate diff. realized — Gain7045011FX gain on clearing → P&L income

Scenario 2: Month-End Valuation (LSBEW / LHBEW) — MOST CRITICAL

When: At month-end, program FAGL_FC_VAL revalues all foreign currency balances at the closing exchange rate. This creates unrealized FX gains/losses that are posted and automatically reversed at the start of the next period. WITHOUT these fields: FAGL_FC_VAL silently skips the account entirely. Cash on the balance sheet remains valued at the WRONG historical rate.

FieldOBA1 LabelUNESCO ValueMeaning
LSBEWValuation — Val.loss 16045011Unrealized FX loss → reversed next period
LHBEWValuation — Val.gain 17045011Unrealized FX gain → reversed next period
CRITICAL GAP: 213 of 219 non-USD bank accounts (10*) have NO T030H valuation config (3% coverage). FAGL_FC_VAL skips these accounts at month-end. FX gains/losses on real cash balances in 80+ currencies across 80+ field offices are NOT reflected in financial statements. See House Bank Config Companion → OBA1 Deep Dive tab for full analysis.

Scenario 3: Balance Sheet Adjustment (LKORR)

When: SAP posts any valuation or realized entry and needs a correction/offsetting account to balance the posting. Standard pattern: LKORR = the account itself (self-referencing).

FieldOBA1 LabelUNESCO ValueMeaning
LKORRBal.sheet adj.1 / Correction acctThe account itself (e.g., 1165424 → LKORR = 1165424)Without this, SAP cannot complete ANY FX posting — transaction fails

What is EBS?

Electronic Bank Statement (EBS) is the process of importing bank statement files (MT940 format) into SAP, automatically posting them to the correct G/L accounts, and reconciling them against existing payment documents. It replaces manual bank reconciliation.

Why it matters: Without EBS, Treasury would have to manually enter every bank statement line. With ~223K bank statement items (FEBEP) and 964K reconciliation entries (FEBRE) in 2024-2026, automation is essential.

Which banks use EBS? 56 of 67 active house banks are configured for EBS (category EBS_CONFIG). All HQ_PAYING banks also have EBS. Only special-purpose banks (investment, inter-agency, pending) and the FO_LOCAL bank (ECO09) may not use EBS for statement import.

ELECTRONIC BANK STATEMENT (EBS) — END-TO-END FLOW STEP 1: FILE ARRIVAL MT940 file arrives from bank via SWIFT Alliance network • MT940 is the international standard for bank statement data (ISO 15022) • Contains: opening balance, each transaction (debit/credit), closing balance • File identifies itself by: bank key (SWIFT/BIC) + physical account number STEP 2: SAP IMPORT (FF_5) FF_5 background job reads the file and needs to answer 2 questions: Q1: Which SAP house bank does this file belong to? Q2: Which G/L account should receive the postings? STEP 3: TABLE LOOKUPS (the config chain) → T028B lookup: bank key (BANKL) + account number (KTONR) → Returns: format type (VGTYP, e.g., XRT940) + cash management key (BNKKO) → Example: BANKL=SP0000001YCB + KTONR=070040004663 → VGTYP=XRT940, BNKKO=UBA01-MZN1 → T035D lookup: DISKB = BNKKO from T028B → Returns: G/L account number (BNKKO = 10xxxxx) → Example: DISKB=UBA01-MZN1 → BNKKO=0001065424 IF T028B ENTRY MISSING: FF_5 cannot identify the bank → file rejected IF T035D ENTRY MISSING: FF_5 cannot determine G/L → posting fails STEP 4: DUAL POSTING → Post to 10xxxxx (main bank account — permanent ledger, NEVER cleared) → Post to 11xxxxx (sub-account / clearing — reconciliation gap) STEP 5: RECONCILIATION (FEBAN) → FEBAN matches 11xxxxx items against payment/invoice documents → When matched: 11xxxxx item is cleared → reconciliation gap shrinks → Unmatched items remain open on 11xxxxx → manual investigation needed KEY INSIGHT: 10xxxxx items are NEVER cleared — they are the bank's permanent ledger 11xxxxx items are the reconciliation gap — should be cleared by matching Open items on 11xxxxx = actual unreconciled difference between SAP and bank

The Dual-Posting Logic — Why 10* and 11*?

Every bank statement line creates TWO postings in SAP. Understanding why is key to understanding reconciliation.

10xxxxx — Bank Account (Permanent Ledger)

The bank's view of reality. Each MT940 line becomes a line item here. These items are NEVER cleared — they are a permanent record of every bank transaction.

Balance on 10* = the bank's statement balance = how much cash is physically in the account.

Gold DB: 223K FEBEP items (2024-2026). The permanent bank ledger.

11xxxxx — Clearing Account (Reconciliation Gap)

SAP's reconciliation workspace. Each bank statement line also posts here, and then FEBAN tries to match (clear) it against the corresponding payment or invoice document.

Open items on 11* = unreconciled bank statement lines = the gap between what the bank says happened and what SAP can explain. Target: minimize to zero.

Gold DB: 964K FEBRE entries. Search strings (Tag 86) used for auto-matching. Algorithm 015 handles 85.7% of cases.

Why this matters for house bank config: The 10* GL (bank account) must be configured in T035D with the correct DISKB key. The 11* GL (clearing) must have XOPVW=X in SKB1 (open item management) to enable clearing. If XOPVW is missing, FEBAN cannot clear items on 11* — they accumulate as false open items forever.

Reconciliation — How FEBAN Clears 11* Items

After EBS posts to 10* and 11*, the reconciliation process (FEBAN) attempts to match each 11* item against existing SAP documents (payment runs, manual postings, vendor invoices). Matching uses several strategies:

Matching MethodHow It WorksCoverage
Auto-clear (Algorithm 015) SAP automatically matches 11* postings against payment documents using document number, amount, and reference fields. This handles the vast majority of cases. 85.7% of incoming items
Search String (Tag 86) MT940 Tag 86 contains the note-to-payee text. SAP extracts reference numbers from this text and matches against open items. Substitution rules in YBASUBST/YTFI_BA_SUBST customize extraction per bank format. Variable — depends on bank's Tag 86 format
Manual FEBAN Treasury analyst opens FEBAN, reviews unmatched 11* items, and manually assigns them to the correct document or creates a new posting. Remaining ~14% that auto-matching cannot resolve

Production reality (Gold DB): 102I posting type = 99.6% of bank statement items (not 29.2% as initially claimed). ACH returns (BELNR=*) account for the remaining edge cases. The EBS processing is highly automated at UNESCO.

Month-End: FX Revaluation of Bank Accounts

At month-end, all non-USD bank account balances must be revalued at the closing exchange rate. This is where T030H (OBA1 configuration) is used. See the OBA1 section in the House Bank Config tab for the complete field template.

MONTH-END FX REVALUATION FAGL_FC_VAL runs for all non-USD accounts → For each G/L account: look up T030H entry → IF T030H MISSING: SILENTLY SKIP (no error, no warning — just skipped) → IF T030H exists: revalue balance at closing rate → Post unrealized FX gain/loss to LSBEW/LHBEW accounts (6045011/7045011) → Post balance sheet adjustment to LKORR (account itself) → Auto-reverse at start of next period CRITICAL GAP: 213 of 219 non-USD bank accounts (10*) have NO T030H → 3% coverage These accounts hold real cash in 80+ currencies at 80+ field offices FAGL_FC_VAL silently skips them → FX gains/losses NOT in financial statements → Material financial reporting gap

What is the Payment Process?

UNESCO uses SAP's F110 automatic payment program to pay vendors, employees, and partners. F110 reads open invoices, determines which bank to pay from based on currency and payment method, groups items into payment documents, generates XML payment files (DMEE), and sends them to the bank. The bank confirms execution via MT940 bank statements, which are then reconciled via EBS.

Who runs it: HQ Treasury runs F110 weekly (the "Wednesday cycle" — C_LOPEZ and I_MARQUAND are the primary operators). Field offices with local disbursement banks (e.g., ECO09 in Mozambique) pay locally by check.

Scale: 550K payment cases, $16.8B total (2024-2026). 9 company codes (IBE, ICBA, ICTP, IIEP, MGIE, UBO, UIL, UIS, UNES).

Payment Processing Flow — End to End

What Are YBANK Sets and Why Do They Matter?

YBANK sets are the backbone of UNESCO's cash management reporting. They are GS02 account sets (SETLEAF, class 0000) that group bank G/L accounts (10* only) by currency and location (HQ vs field office). Every cash position report, average balance calculation, and treasury dashboard depends on these sets being complete and accurate.

What happens when an account is missing from a YBANK set? That bank account's balance is invisible to Treasury reporting. Cash position reports show an incomplete picture. Average balance calculations exclude the account. The field office appears to have less cash than it actually has. This is why step 10 (GS02) in the house bank creation process is critical — forgetting to add the new account to the correct set means it disappears from all reports.

Who maintains them: DBS configurators (during house bank creation) and Treasury team (periodic reviews). 158 entries across 10 sets as of 2026-04-08. D01 and P01 are aligned via GRW_SET transport (established 2026-04-07).
