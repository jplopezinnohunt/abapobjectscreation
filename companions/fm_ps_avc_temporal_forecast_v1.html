SAP FM offers 5+ transaction types that mutate fund state; only 3 propagate symmetrically to PS via the 10-digit hard link. The 6,232 (Fund, WBS) pairs in BAD state are not 6,232 separate problems; they are 6,232 manifestations of one missing contract.

-- Active WBS only (status I0002 REL, not CLSD/TECO/DLFL)
CREATE TEMP TABLE _active_wbs AS
SELECT DISTINCT j.OBJNR FROM jest j
WHERE j.STAT='I0002' AND j.INACT=''
  AND j.OBJNR LIKE 'PR%'
  AND j.OBJNR NOT IN (SELECT OBJNR FROM jest WHERE STAT IN ('I0045','I0046','I0076') AND INACT='');

-- Fund-from-WBS derivation (HARD-link first, fall back to coep history)
CREATE TEMP TABLE _wbs_fund_map AS
SELECT c.OBJNR,
  COALESCE(
    (SELECT FINCODE FROM funds WHERE FINCODE = SUBSTR(p.POSID, 1, 10) AND FIKRS='UNES'),
    (SELECT GEBER FROM coep WHERE OBJNR=c.OBJNR AND GEBER!='' GROUP BY GEBER ORDER BY COUNT(*) DESC LIMIT 1)
  ) as fund_derived,
  CASE WHEN EXISTS (...hard link check...) THEN 'HARD' ELSE 'DERIVED' END as link_type
FROM (SELECT DISTINCT OBJNR FROM cooi WHERE GJAHR='2026') c
LEFT JOIN prps_full p ON c.OBJNR = p.OBJNR;

FIX-F: Meta-AVC architecture — the structural answer

User architectural insight (Session #69): "entre FM y PS debe ser un meta-AVC combinando los 2"

Insight: every path-specific fix leaves at least one source of misalignment unguarded. UNESCO has at least 4 paths that mutate fund state — MULESOFT (automated, budget side), FB01 (manual, real posting), FMBB/FMBBT (manual, budget side), and FMX1/FMRES (manual, reserves). A fix on any single path leaves the others uncontrolled.

The Meta-AVC architecture

Replace the two independent AVC engines with a single unified validation layer that holds combined FM+PS state and runs at every posting:

The broken implicit contract — why PS-AVC stays positive while FM-AVC empties

The structural insight (verified TIER_1 on 2026-05-01): UNESCO's biennium close-out moves revenue from the legacy fund to the successor fund, but does not move the corresponding open obligations (POs, draft SES, pending invoices). The implicit Fund↔Project relationship is established at project creation time and is enforced by the 10-digit hard link, but is NOT enforced when revenue is reallocated.

Why every biennium close-out re-runs the same play

1
New biennium fund created. January of biennium-start year (2024-01 for current cohort), MULESOFT integration provisions ~100 new TYPE=104 funds in a single batch. Each fund gets a top-WBS auto-created, no operational FIPEX budget loaded.
2
Revenue loaded onto FIPEX=REVENUE placeholder. Donor cash-inflow integration posts revenue (WRTTP=66) against a single placeholder commitment item called REVENUE. Operational FIPEX (11 personnel, 13 subcontracts, 20 travel, 30 equipment, …) get $0 budget by construction.
3
Spending happens on operational FIPEX. 24 months of POs, SES, invoices post against operational FIPEX. FM-AVC sees deficit on each operational bucket but silently allows postings because the AVC strategy aggregates against the FUND-level revenue (which is positive, sitting on REVENUE placeholder).
4
Biennium-end revenue REVERSED for transfer to successor. TIER_1 On 2026-01-31, A_BANSAL posts a 257-document FB01 batch from BSP-emails. Revenue on FIPEX=REVENUE goes from positive to negative as it's pulled toward the successor fund (196EAR4043). Aggregate AVC pool now negative.
5
Operational FIPEX shows deficit → AVC blocks. Next time a field office attempts SES / MIRO / F110 against the legacy fund, FM-AVC validation fires FMAVC005 "insufficient budget" — even though the WBS-level PS-AVC pool may still report healthy. The 3 Gabon POs (4500543365 / 4500540022 / 4500540024) are 3 of an estimated 2,000+ POs in this state across UNESCO.
The play is structural: nothing in steps 1-5 is a bug. It's a working configuration that produces predictable failure at biennium-end. Fixing this requires either (A) loading revenue onto operational FIPEX from day 1 (changes the procurement-cash-allocation procedure), or (B) aligning PS-AVC granularity with FM-AVC (config decision in TKA01 / OPSV).
