---

## The headline

In scope 2024–2026, **3,359 BCM payment batches** representing **~$656M in local-currency exposure** were created and approved by the **same user** (`CRUSR = CHUSR`) — a dual-control violation on face. However, the root cause is **not fraud or intent**; it is **structural automation debt at HQ treasury**.

**70.3% of these batches run on Wednesdays.** This is the signature of a weekly, manual AP payment cycle executed by a handful of named humans.

**Key reclassifications from what was previously reported:**

1. **The prior "top risk" was wrong.** A hypothesis document drafted in Session #036 flagged F_DERAKHSHAN's 259 solo PAYROLL batches as the highest concern. The full data shows F_DERAKHSHAN has a second approver **74% of the time**. His 161 solo batches are the exception (vacation backup), not the rule. Payroll dual-control in UNESCO is broadly working.

2. **The real top risk is C_LOPEZ + I_MARQUAND.** Together they account for **2,705 batches (81% of the total) and ~$475M in exposure**, and they self-approve **93–95% of the time**. The reason is structural: **they are the only two HQ treasury operators**. When one covers the other's vacation, there is literally no third person to provide dual control. Hiring a third operator or automating the Wednesday AP cycle would collapse the number.

3. **These are humans, not service accounts.** Verified: none of the four top users owns any background job in `tbtco`. They are dialog users manually executing F110/BCM every week. The "high volume same-user" signature was initially suspected to be automation; it is not.

4. **The gap is growing, not shrinking.** Session #027 (2025-early) reported 3,394 same-user batches. Session #037 (2026-04-05) measures 4,760 all-time. **Delta = +1,366 batches (+40%)** accumulated in the 15 sessions during which the finding existed in the PMO tracker without an action plan. This is the cost of documented inaction.

---

## Highest-risk subset

Of the 3,359 batches, **331 belong to `UNES_AP_EX`** — the Exception List for payments to AE, JO, and embargo-screened countries. The dollar amount is small (**~$1.9M**) but the sanctions exposure is not. A single-user approval on an AE/JO batch means no independent verification of a payment touching a sanctions jurisdiction. This is the subset that should be fixed first regardless of the broader remediation choice.

## What a reasonable CFO would ask

1. **"Is any of this money actually lost?"** — No evidence of loss. This is a control-design gap, not a fraud report. The monitoring script enables detection if something does happen.
2. **"Why did this take 15 sessions to surface?"** — Structural. The finding existed but had no pre-committed deliverable and no gate forcing it to ship. Session #037 redesigned the session open/close protocol (`.agents/workflows/session_start.md` v2) to prevent this class of drift. The symmetry control is now executable (`scripts/session_preflight.py`).
3. **"Why are C_LOPEZ and I_MARQUAND still the only two?"** — This is the question for HR/Treasury, not IT. The IT side can only flag the exposure and give the numbers. **Staffing is the lever.**
4. **"Can I get this as a daily email?"** — Yes. Path 1 above. The script already emits CSV + JSON + HTML. Wiring to an SMTP or a scheduled job is a one-day task.
